How We Play

These aren't posted in a contract, but it's how we've been playing for 20 years.
It's how we keep it good for everyone.

Must Know

  • You must be on time for the start of the games. After our intro speech and then once teams are set, latecomers can't join. You will be marked as a no-show.
  • No cleats, no exceptions. Turf shoes or sneakers.
  • No slide tackles, hard tackles, or high kicks. Aggressive players will be asked to leave or ban from the group.
  • Stay for the entire two hours, unless you're injured.
  • Three no-shows in a season means a ban for the rest of that season.
  • You'll be playing alongside people of all backgrounds. Different skill levels, different countries, different ways of knowing the game. Be kind, patient, and supportive of everyone on the field.

On the Ball

  • Every person should be included in our playing, no matter who they are.
  • Don't "showboat" and carry the ball, dribble around everyone, score on your own. That's cool other places, not here.
  • Keep your feet low. No high kicks, no hard shots.

Give People Room

  • If a beginner has the ball, back off. Give them space to figure it out. Don't "help" by playing their position for them.
  • Pass to them even if they keep missing it.
  • At the start of each game, beginners and recently injured players raise their hand. That's your signal to give them extra space, no contact.
  • Be mindful of physical contact, especially in coed play. Men, be aware of your size and strength. Don't be overly physical with women and nonbinary players.

Our Vibe

  • Keep your energy mellow, even against equally skilled players.
  • No one should feel that they need to call a foul or end up on the ground. That's a sign the pace is too fast.
  • Rotate positions between games. Don't camp the same spot all day.
  • Women, Trans, and Nonbinary (WTNB) players and men should be spread across positions, not clustered together.
  • Advanced players, it's on you to make sure everyone feels included.
  • If someone says they're injured, respect it immediately, no questions asked.
  • Let people make mistakes. If someone accidentally does a handball to cover their face, don't sweat it. If someone does a throw-in wrong, don't yell at them.
  • We play rain or shine. (Thunderstorms are different though, we don't mess with lightning.)

After the Game

  • After every game, we head to the Adirondack. Come join us — it's half the fun.
